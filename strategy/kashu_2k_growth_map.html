The Conversation with Adam
Adam is an OG in banking. He sees infrastructure, not hype. Walk him through the tools as proof of execution, then pivot to the channels where his network moves the needle. The goal isn't to impress him -- it's to activate him.
The Specific Asks
1
Community bank and credit union introductions. Who in Adam's network runs business banking at a community bank or credit union that doesn't have a strong processing referral program? One warm intro = a pipeline of their business clients.
2
ISO and agent network connections. Does he know any small ISOs or independent agents who are unhappy with their current processor relationships? These conversations move portfolios, not individual merchants.
3
Franchise and multi-location contacts. Any franchise operators or multi-location business owners in his circle who might benefit from consolidated processing at better rates?
4
PE fund introductions. Does he know fund managers who could benefit from the managed service model? Kashu handles processing across their portfolio companies -- retainer + rev share. The C2C product adds a second value layer (avoids capital calls).
5
Portfolio acquisition sourcing. Any retiring agents or ISOs looking to sell their merchant books? Adam's network might know people looking to exit who'd rather sell to someone who will take care of their merchants.
6
Stress test the plan. Where does he see the biggest risk? What would he do differently? His experience can surface blind spots we can't see from the inside.
How to Frame the Meeting
Open with the tools. Let him click through the statement analyzer, the competitor tool, the dashboard. Show him this isn't PowerPoint -- it's a live operating system. Then pull up this growth map. Walk through the channels tab, show the math, and land on the advisor tab. The close: "Adam, you know the banking side better than anyone. Where in this map can your network accelerate us the fastest?" Let him pick his lane. People invest energy where they feel ownership.
What Adam Needs to Walk Away Thinking
Three things. First: this team executes. The tools aren't concepts, they're built and deployed. Second: the math is real. 15 channels with a 35% buffer isn't wishful thinking, it's risk-managed distribution. Third: his network is the accelerant. He's not just advising -- he's the unlock on the highest-value channels (banks, ISOs, PE). When he leaves, he should already be thinking about who to call first.

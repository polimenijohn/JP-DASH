The Problem

Your portfolio is leaving money on the table every month.

Most PE firms have never looked at payment processing as a coordinated asset. Every portfolio company chose a processor independently. There's no leverage, no visibility, and no upside flowing back to the fund.

No consolidated visibility

You have no aggregate view of what your portfolio companies are paying in processing fees -- across the entire portfolio, it's likely your largest unmanaged recurring expense.

Zero upside from processing volume

Portfolio companies process hundreds of thousands per month. Every dollar goes to a third-party processor. None of it benefits the fund.

Capital calls for working capital gaps

Struggling portfolio companies come back to the fund for capital when cash flow gets tight. That dilutes returns, creates LP friction, and often funds a problem that could have been solved with existing credit.

⏳

Time-consuming portfolio support

Identifying which companies need operational help, then executing on it, pulls operating partner time away from the deals that move the needle.

The Opportunity

Two tracks. Both compound over time.

Kashu addresses two distinct problems in your portfolio simultaneously, through a single partnership.
Track 1 — Merchant Processing

Processing volume becomes fund revenue.

Kashu converts your portfolio's existing payment processing into a revenue-sharing asset. Companies save on fees. The fund earns a revenue share on every dollar processed. Kashu handles all outreach, conversion, and onboarding.

  • Guaranteed better rates for portfolio companies -- in writing
  • Zero downtime on the switch. No missed transactions.
  • Surcharge option eliminates processing costs entirely for eligible companies
  • Revenue share flows to the fund monthly
  • Kashu manages the entire process -- you make the introduction
Track 2 — Credit to Cash

Struggling companies access liquidity without a capital call.

Portfolio companies with available business credit can convert it to cash through Kashu's platform -- covering payroll, vendor payments, operating expenses, and more. No new debt. No fund injection.

  • Converts available business credit to cash, same day
  • Eliminates or reduces the need for capital calls on working capital
  • Keeps LP relationships clean -- no dilution for operational gaps
  • Affiliate commission structure provides an additional fund revenue line
  • Separate product, separate agreement, separate pitch

Compliance & Transparency

Built to hold up in diligence.

Important disclosures for fund counsel review:

The revenue share arrangement between Kashu and the fund requires full disclosure to each portfolio company prior to signing a Kashu processing agreement. Portfolio company management must be informed that the fund receives a revenue share on their processing volume. This is not a condition of the fund relationship -- each portfolio company independently chooses whether to engage Kashu.

  • Fund counsel should review the revenue share structure for compliance with fiduciary duty obligations and fund governance documents
  • Revenue share may be structured as a fund-level benefit or routed through the management company, depending on fund governance -- Kashu is flexible on the payment destination
  • Each portfolio company signs its own independent Kashu merchant agreement
  • No portfolio company is obligated to switch processors as a condition of the fund relationship
  • All Kashu marketing materials used with portfolio companies require pre-approval per standard affiliate/partner protocols

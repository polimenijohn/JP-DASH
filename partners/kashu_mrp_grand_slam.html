MRP Recruiting Close Script

Step 1

The Opener

"Hey [Partner], appreciate you hopping on. Quick context -- I'm the CRO at Kashu, we're a payment processing platform. If it makes sense we'll talk next steps, if not no hard feelings. Cool?"

Why it works: Pattern interrupt. Most MRP recruiting pitches sound like MLM. This is direct and low-pressure.
Step 2

The Problem (Teach)

"Here's what most people don't realize about payment processing. Every business in America processes credit cards. The average business does $60K/month. Their processor takes 2.5-3.5% of every transaction. That's $1,500 to $2,100 per month going to some faceless processor. Most of those businesses are overpaying and don't know it. And here's the thing -- nobody is telling them. Their processor sure isn't going to volunteer that information."

"You know [X number] of business owners personally. How many of them do you think have actually looked at their processing statement in the last year? Almost none. That's the gap."

Why it works: Establishes the market opportunity without pitching. Creates awareness of a problem their network has.
Step 3

The Offer

"What we do at Kashu is simple. We help businesses lower their processing costs -- usually dramatically. We can do surcharge programs where the merchant pays literally zero in processing fees and actually adds a revenue line. The switch takes days, not weeks, and the merchant doesn't miss a single transaction."

"Now here's where you come in. You know business owners. You have relationships I'll never have. All I need from you is a warm introduction -- a text, an email, a 'hey you should talk to John.' That's it. I handle the call, I handle the close, I handle the onboarding. You just connect us."

"For every merchant you refer that goes live, you earn residual income. Every month. For as long as they process. It's not a one-time referral fee -- it's recurring income that compounds."

Why it works: Simple value prop. Emphasizes that the MRP doesn't need to sell or know anything about processing. The "warm introduction" bar is intentionally low.

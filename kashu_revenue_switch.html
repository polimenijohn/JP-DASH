KNOW YOUR ENEMY

Pick their processor. Hit the weak points. Close the deal.

Square

Flat rate 2.6% + $0.10 means they're massively overpaying on debit and small tickets
No dedicated support -- try calling Square when something breaks
Account holds and freezes with zero warning -- Square is notorious for holding funds
Talk Track

"Square is built for the farmer's market, not a real business doing your volume. You're paying the same rate whether someone swipes a debit card or an Amex Black. On your volume that's costing you [$X/month] more than it should."

Stripe

2.9% + $0.30 online is one of the highest in the industry
Built for developers, not business owners -- you shouldn't need an engineer to manage payments
No in-person processing solution worth using
Talk Track

"Stripe is incredible technology wrapped in the worst pricing in the industry. At your volume, you're paying a premium for features you don't use. And if you ever need to talk to a human? Good luck."

Toast

Locked into Toast hardware -- proprietary equipment you can't take with you
Processing rates are buried in the contract and typically 2.49% + $0.15 or higher
Monthly software fees on top of processing fees ($0-$165+/month per terminal)
Talk Track

"Toast is a software company that happens to process payments. You're paying for their POS system AND overpaying on processing. Those are two separate decisions and they've bundled them so you can't see what you're actually paying per transaction."

Clover

Owned by Fiserv (one of the biggest processors) -- you're paying middleman markup
Equipment is expensive ($600-$1,800 per terminal) and proprietary
Rates vary wildly because it's sold through resellers, most merchants don't know their real rate
Talk Track

"Clover looks modern but it's Fiserv underneath -- one of the biggest processors in the world marking up your rate through a middleman. Let me show you what you're actually paying vs. what you could be paying."

Heartland

Acquired by Global Payments -- massive corporate processor, you're a number
Known for long-term contracts with early termination fees
Sales reps promise low rates then fees creep up after 6 months
Talk Track

"Heartland was a great processor 10 years ago. Then Global Payments bought them and turned it into a contract machine. Let me guess -- your rate looked great when you signed but your last few statements tell a different story?"

Worldpay

One of the largest processors in the world -- zero personalization
Complex pricing tiers (interchange-plus with hidden markups)
Notoriously difficult to cancel -- long contracts, ETFs up to $500
Talk Track

"Worldpay processes for millions of merchants. You'll never talk to the same person twice. And if you try to leave, they'll hit you with a cancellation fee. We handle your cancellation for you -- that's included."

PayPal

2.99% + $0.49 per transaction is highway robbery
Account limitations and holds are common -- they can freeze your money for 180 days
Not a real merchant processor -- it's a payment facilitator with consumer-grade support
Talk Track

"PayPal is not a processor. It's a digital wallet that lets you accept payments as a side feature. At your volume, you're leaving [$X/month] on the table and one algorithmic flag from having your funds frozen for 6 months."

Elavon

Owned by US Bank -- big bank processor with big bank problems
Hidden fees (PCI non-compliance fees, statement fees, batch fees)
Pricing is opaque -- most merchants can't read their own statement
Talk Track

"Elavon buries fees in places you'd never think to look. PCI compliance fees, batch fees, statement fees -- it adds up to hundreds per month that aren't in your 'rate.' That's why I do the Statement Autopsy -- let me show you what's hiding in your statement."

The Opener
Pattern Interrupt

What to Say
"Hey [Name], appreciate you jumping on. I'm gonna be straight with you -- I'm not here to give you a sales pitch about payment processing. That would put us both to sleep. Why It Works

Pattern interrupt. Every processor rep opens with a pitch. You're opening with honesty and a curiosity hook. Sets the frame that you're different.

2
The Statement Autopsy
Teach
▾
What to Say
"So I pulled your statement apart. Here's what jumped out..." [Screen share the statement analysis or savings report] "You're paying ___% effective rate right now. That's $___ per month going straight to your processor. Most of that is negotiable, and some of it is just markup you shouldn't be paying at all. Did you know you were paying this much?"
Then pause. Let them react. They almost never know their actual effective rate.
Why It Works

Challenger Sale "Teach" moment. You're showing them something about their own business they didn't know. This builds authority and creates the gap between where they are and where they could be.

3
The Reframe
Pattern Interrupt #2
▾
What to Say
"Here's the thing though -- I'm not actually here to save you money. Every processor rep says that. What I want to show you is how to turn processing from a cost you manage into a revenue line you profit from. Those are two completely different conversations."
Why It Works

This is the line that separates you from every other rep. Inversion. Nobody else says this. The merchant has never heard processing described as a revenue line. Their brain has to recategorize what you're offering.

6
The Guarantee
Reverse Risk
▾
What to Say
"Now here's the part where I put my money where my mouth is. Before you sign anything, your guaranteed rate is in writing. If we can't beat what you're paying right now -- on paper, in black and white -- we don't deserve your business and I won't waste another minute of your time." "And if at any point in the first 90 days your effective rate isn't exactly what we promised, we'll cut you a check for the difference and release you from any obligation. No questions, no runaround." "I wouldn't make that offer if I wasn't dead certain about the numbers we just looked at."
Why It Works

Risk reversal. The merchant has zero downside. The confidence statement at the end ("I wouldn't make that offer if...") is implicit social proof -- you've done this enough times to guarantee the outcome.

The Close
The No-Brainer
▾
What to Say
"So let me just lay it out. You get a guaranteed lower rate in writing. You go live without missing a day of business. And surcharge turns processing from something you pay for into something that pays you. We handle the entire switch, the equipment, the onboarding, even the breakup with your current processor." "Cool. I'll send the application right now. Takes about 10 minutes and we handle the rest."
If They Hesitate
"What's the one thing holding you back?" (Then address it directly, add a bonus if needed per Hormozi's 1-on-1 bonus technique)
If "I Need to Think About It"
"Totally get it. What specifically do you want to think about? Because if it's the rate, that's locked in writing. If it's the transition, we handle all of it. If it's the risk, you've got 90 days to prove us wrong and we'll write you a check. So what's the actual thing?"
Why It Works

The summary close restates the three-part kill cleanly. The pauses create space for the merchant to close themselves. The objection handling doesn't accept "I need to think about it" at face value -- it isolates the real objection (Gap Selling) and addresses it with components of the offer that already exist.
